States in Motion

We have updated, expanded and simplified States in Motion, a series of  graphs and charts that explore and compare states' education-related data over time. “States in Motion” explores 13  datasets, including National Assessment of Education Progress (NAEP) test scores, teacher salaries and student enrollments in all 50 states and the District of Columbia. In our update, we have included information on student poverty. The interstate comparisons provide answers to questions that include: How does California’s investment in public education compare with that of other states? How has that investment changed over the decades? How does the level of spending look when compared with datasets like a state’s per-capita income, proportion of students in poverty, teacher salaries and students-per-teacher? And what’s the correlation, if any, between state spending on education and student test scores as well as between test scores and percentage of students in poverty? Data for many of the charts go back to 1970; data on tests scores on the National Assessment of  Educational Progress or NAEP are more recent. Clicking the Download Data tab in the lower left of each chart will provide an Excel sheet containing the full data for the states by year. We remain interested in hearing your suggestions for making States in Motion  more useful or understandable. You can also share your comments at the bottom of the page or send an email. The project was conceived and developed by Jeff Camp, who made it available to EdSource for wider distribution and comment. Jeff chairs the education work of Full Circle Fund, a nonprofit volunteer organization with contributing members from all over the Bay Area. He is also designed Ed100, which offers a set of primers about education in California; it's particularly useful for parents seeking a short course on the state's new funding and governing systems. Ready to begin? To compare states, just click on the state's name in the key. California will be in blue. All the data used for this project is available online here, including sources and methodology.
